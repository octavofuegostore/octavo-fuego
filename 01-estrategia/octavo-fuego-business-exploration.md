# Business Plan Exploration: Octavo Fuego
## Ecommerce of Traditional & Sacred Medicines

---

## Executive Summary

**Octavo Fuego** (Eighth Fire) is positioned to enter the sacred medicines market with a unique value proposition rooted in the Anishinaabe prophecy. This exploration analyzes the viability of building an ecommerce platform for traditional indigenous medicines, specifically targeting the Colombian market with international expansion potential.

**Key Finding**: The market presents a significant opportunity for a boutique brand that combines ethical sourcing, spiritual storytelling, and premium positioning. Success depends on navigating regulatory complexities, establishing authentic indigenous partnerships, and differentiating from established competitors.

## 3. Legal & Regulatory Framework

### 3.1 Colombia-Specific Considerations

**Current Legal Workaround**:
- Products sold as "**Nasal Incense**" or "**Aromatherapy Products**"
- Avoid terms: "medicine," "tobacco," "rapé," "snuff"
- Labeling: "For external use only - not for consumption"

**Regulatory Bodies to Monitor**:
- **INVIMA** (National Institute for Food and Drug Surveillance)
- **Ministry of Health** - tobacco regulations
- **Customs (DIAN)** - import/export classification
- **Procuraduría** - consumer protection

### 3.2 Import/Export Regulations

**Importing to Colombia**:
```
Brazil (Acre) → Colombia
Classification: Natural products / Herbal preparations
HS Code: 1211.90 (Plants for perfumes/pharmacy)
Required: Phytosanitary certificate, commercial invoice
Risk Level: LOW (if properly declared as non-consumable)
```

**Exporting from Colombia**:
```
Colombia → International
More complex - requires export permits
Recommendation: Partner with established exporter initially
Consider: Katukina model - EU base with Brazilian roots
```

### 3.3 Risk Mitigation Strategies

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| Regulatory change | Medium | High | Legal monitoring, diversify product categories |
| Customs seizure | Low | Medium | Proper documentation, small batch imports |
| Consumer health claims | Medium | High | Strict no-medical-claims policy |
| Indigenous rights disputes | Low | High | Fair trade agreements, transparency |

### 3.4 Recommended Legal Structure

**Company Type**: SAS (Simplified Stock Company)
**RUT Activity Codes**:
- 4791 - Retail via internet
- 4773 - Herbalist/pharmacy products
- 4649 - Wholesale of other products

**Additional Requirements**:
- Sanitary registration for "aromatherapy products"
- Chamber of Commerce registration
- ICA authorization if importing plant materials

### 4.3 Inventory Management

**Initial Inventory Strategy**:
- **MOQ**: 100-200g per rapé variety
- **Initial SKUs**: 5-8 rapé varieties + 2 sananga + accessories
- **Investment**: ~$3,000-5,000 initial stock
- **Turnover Target**: 60-90 days

- Airtight packaging to preserve freshness

- **Express**: Mensajero Urbano, Rappi (Bogotá same-day)
- **Cost**: $5-15 depending on destination
- **Free shipping threshold**: Orders >$100

**International (Future)**:

| **Reputation damage** | Low | Critical | Medium | Quality control, authentic messaging |
| **Cash flow problems** | Medium | High | High | Conservative inventory, pre-orders |

### 7.2 Specific Mitigation Strategies

**Regulatory Risk**:
- Maintain relationships with 2-3 lawyers specializing in natural products
- Diversify into non-regulated accessories (30% of catalog)
- Monitor INVIMA publications monthly
- Join industry associations for advocacy

**Supply Chain Risk**:
- Maintain 60-90 days inventory buffer
- Develop relationships with 3+ communities
- Document alternative suppliers
- Consider Colombian indigenous alternatives (Kogi, Arhuaco)

**Financial Risk**:
- Start with consignment model where possible
- Offer pre-orders for new products
- Maintain 6-month operating expense reserve
- Avoid debt in first 12 months
